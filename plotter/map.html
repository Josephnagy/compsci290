Defense Spending Data Story

This data visualization models the ratio of every country’s annual defense spending budget (in USD) compared to the number of citizens they have below the poverty line. The purpose of this visualization is to bring into question the amount of money countries spend on military defense and suggest such money is used to help lift their citizens out of poverty. This data “story” does not support or suggest any specific policy solutions to this phenomenon, but instead serves to investigate the relationship between the two values and how it varies across nations.

The radius of every blue cirlce on the map is directly proportional to the defense-to-poverty-ratio which is calcualted by dividing a country's defense spending budget (in USD) by the number of citizens living below the poverty line.
